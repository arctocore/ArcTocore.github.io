MusicBand Studio Pro — how it works
Overview

A full band studio
that runs in your browser.

MusicBand Studio Pro turns a single web page into a playable studio: real sampled instruments, a step sequencer, a mixer with a master effects rack, whole-song generation in the style of famous artists, sample recording, phase-vocoder pitch & stretch, and one-click WAV / MP3 export. This guide walks through the whole flow — from pressing Start Audio to exporting a finished track.

Real instruments

Drums, guitars, piano, strings, brass, woodwinds, organ and synth keys — streamed as real audio samples with synth fallbacks.

Generate songs

Randomize a full arrangement in the style of 130+ featured artists, each with its own original signature melody, groove and progression.

Mix & shape

A per-track mixer plus a master effects rack, sound types, amp tones and guitar distortion sculpt the sound.

Phase vocoder

Shift pitch and stretch time independently on samples and on the final export, powered by PhaseVocoder.js.

Quick Start

From silence to a finished track

Follow these steps in order — the studio is designed to be used from top to bottom.

1

Start the audio engine

Click Start Audio in the top toolbar. Browsers only allow sound after a click, so this unlocks the engine and fades the master in cleanly.

2

Pick an instrument

Choose a card in Band Instruments. Play it with the on-screen piano, the keyboard keys A–K, or the chord pads.

3

Generate or build a song

Press Randomize Song to create a full arrangement in an artist's style, or build your own by adding parts to the Loop Sequencer.

4

Arrange on the timeline

Every track is a block you can trim, expand and move, just like clips in a modern DAW.

5

Shape the sound

Balance levels in the Mixer, dial in the Effects Rack, and adjust global Pitch and Stretch in the toolbar.

6

Record, export & save

Record a performance, export a WAV or MP3, and save the whole project to a file so you can reload it later.

Interface Tour

The panels, one by one

The studio is organised into collapsible panels. Here is what each one does.

Top toolbar

Transport (Play, Stop, Record, Click, Clear), Save / Load Project, and global sliders: Tempo, Master, Octave, plus phase-vocoder Pitch & Stretch.

Band Instruments

Real sampled instruments plus a Sound Type selector, per-instrument amp tone, and a distortion selector for electric guitar.

Song Presets

One-tap patterns that load a ready-made groove so you can start playing straight away.

Selected & Piano

The performance area: playable pads, a fully polyphonic piano keyboard, Record Sample, Record Mic, Generate Music, Export Sample, and a multi-instrument Sample Sequence (8 / 16 / 32 steps) under Amp Tone.

Effects Rack

The master effects, sitting under the piano: Reverb, Delay, Feedback, Chorus, Drive, Filter, Crush and Swing.

Mixer & Sequencer

A full-width mixer with a fader, mute and solo per track, and a bar-based Loop Sequencer for the whole song.

The piano is a standard polyphonic keyboard: hold several keys together to sound a chord, and each note stops the moment you release its key. Press Stop Playing to release everything at once.

Make Music

Generate, in the style of the greats

MusicBand can build whole songs and short samples for you. Nothing is copied from real recordings — each of the 130+ artists is an original blend of tempo, groove and chord progression, titled after one of their real top-5 songs. The studio chooses a fresh tone and writes a brand-new lead melody on its own every time, so no two renders ever sound the same.

Randomize Song

Pick an artist or genre, or leave it on “Surprise Me”. The studio lays down drums, bass, chords, an optional pad and a fresh lead across the timeline, and titles it after one of that artist's real top-5 songs.

Each Randomize picks a different tone (Sound Type) and writes a brand-new melody on its own — so even the same artist never sounds the same twice.

Generate Music sample

Generate Music renders a short clip for the selected instrument in the Style you choose beside Amp Tone — a specific artist or “Surprise Me” for a random one — letting the system pick a fresh tone and melody each time, names it after one of that artist's top songs, and saves it as a new sample you can drop on the timeline.

The system chooses the Sound Type for you and layers it with your amp tone and guitar distortion, so every clip sounds a little different.

Sound Types

Swap an instrument's real timbre for a synth voice — electronic, techno, synth pad, retro, FM, supersaw and more.

Amp Tones

Drive plus a three-band EQ, with 34 voicings from Classic Rock and Jazz to Metal, Djent and Lo-Fi.

Guitar Distortion

The electric guitar adds distortion of sizes, angles, distances and directions — drive, bit-crush, space and stereo movement.

Timeline & Regions

Arrange like a modern DAW

The Loop Sequencer is a bar-based timeline. Every part drops as a block sized to its own sound length — a phrase, a groove or a sample clip — so you can shape the arrangement the way you would in Soundtrap or GarageBand.

Trim

Drag either edge of a block inward to shorten which part of the track plays.

Expand

Drag an edge outward — past the end and the song grows to make room automatically.

Move

Grab the block's header to slide the whole part — its notes travel with it.

Copy & Paste

Duplicate a block with its copy button, or select it and press Ctrl+C then Ctrl+V.

Add or remove bars, set a per-clip tempo, and edit any step cell by clicking it. While a song is still auto-generated, the top Play button spins up a fresh random title each time — as soon as you edit a block, it becomes yours and Play just plays it.

Record & Phase Vocoder

Capture and reshape sound

Record the studio's own output or your microphone into a sample slot, then bend it with the global phase vocoder.

Record & upload samples

Rec Sample captures whatever the studio is playing; Record Mic captures your microphone; and you can upload a WAV or MP3. Each lands in its own slot with a fader and channel — and an uploaded track drops straight onto the timeline at bar 1, so every upload plays in sync with the song.

Pitch & Stretch

The toolbar's Pitch and Stretch sliders drive PhaseVocoder.js: Pitch shifts by semitones while keeping length, and Stretch changes length while keeping pitch — applied to your samples and to the final export.

Sample-sequence drum machine

Under Amp Tone, tap Add to layer instruments as rows in one grid, pick a length of 8, 16 or 32 steps, then program each row. Choose a tone first — click a drum pad (Kick, Snare, Hi-Hat…) or play a chord on any instrument (C major, then G major…) — and each Add inserts that exact tone as its own row. So you can stack two piano chords, three drum pieces and a bassline together. Press Play to loop it all, and Clear to reset. Every instrument on a step is locked to the same beat on the audio clock, so the groove stays perfectly in sync.

Sample & loop stay out of each other's way

Sample playback and the Loop Sequencer are mutually exclusive: starting a sample — Play Sample, Generate Music or the step-sequencer — stops the loop, and starting the loop stops the sample.

Scratch Sampler

Select any instrument — piano, drums, a synth or a recorded sample — and a visual vinyl turntable appears just above Amp Tone, loaded with that instrument's OWN sound. Spin the record to scratch the instrument you picked; the platter turns to follow your hand, and turning it backwards plays in reverse. It runs through the instrument's channel, so your Sound Type, amp tone and effects come with it. Let go mid-spin and the record free-spins and coasts to a stop.

Export & Save

Take your track with you

WAV & MP3

Render the whole song — or a single sample — to a downloadable WAV or MP3, encoded right in your browser.

Save Project

Save every setting and track to a project file: tempo, effects, sound type, amp tone, distortion and the timeline.

Load Project

Reload a project — or a shared settings file — any time. Recorded audio is stored in your browser.

Export Settings

Share your studio setup: Export Settings saves your tempo, tone, amp, effects and pitch/stretch as a small JSON file that other producers can load to match your sound.

1

Start the audio engine

Click Start Audio in the top transport to unlock the browser's sound. The status light turns to Ready, and Play, Record and the keyboard come alive.

2

Pick an instrument

Choose a sound in the Band Instruments rack — grand piano, drums, guitar, strings, brass or a synth. Your pick drives the piano keys, the pads and the Generate Music button.

3

Build a loop

Press Randomize Song for a full arrangement in a Style you choose, or play the on-screen piano and pads. Generate Music renders a melodic clip in the selected Style and Sound Type.

4

Arrange in the timeline

Drag a block to move it, drag either edge to trim or expand, and set a per-clip tempo. Duplicate a part with its copy button, or select it and press Ctrl+C then Ctrl+V to paste.

5

Keep it tight

Clips only sound inside their block. A sample now stops at the end of its region instead of ringing out over the empty bars that follow, so only the active music plays.

6

Record & shape the sound

Hit Record to capture a take over the loop; stopping opens the Export Music window. Shape the tone with Amp Tone, Distortion, the Effects Rack and the global Pitch / Stretch sliders.

7

Export or save

Download the finished mix as WAV or MP3, or Save Project to keep every setting and track so you can pick up right where you left off.

8

Choose a Sound Type

In Band Instruments, swap an instrument's real timbre for a synth voice — electronic, techno, synth pad, retro, FM and more. It reshapes both live playing and what you generate.

9

Dial an Amp Tone

Beside the instrument, pick an Amp Tone — drive plus a three-band EQ with 34 voicings from Classic Rock and Jazz to Metal and Lo-Fi. It colours every note through that channel.

10

Add guitar distortion

With the electric guitar selected, the Distortion menu adds sizes, angles, distances and directions — drive, bit-crush, space and stereo movement for a full guitar rig.

11

Open the Sample Sequence

Under Amp Tone, the Sample Sequence is a mini drum machine. Tap Add to layer several instruments as rows in one grid and program each independently.

12

Pick a sequence length

Use the Steps selector to make the pattern 8, 16 or 32 steps long. Changing length keeps whatever you have already programmed.

13

Insert drum tones

Click a drum pad — Kick, then Snare, then Hi-Hat — and each Add inserts that exact drum tone as its own row, so you build a kit one piece at a time.

14

Play chords into rows

Play a chord on any instrument (a pad or held keys) and the next Add inserts that exact chord as its own row — stack a C major and a G major piano row, a bassline and drums together.

15

Switch Style live

While the loop is playing, choose a new Style in the Loop Sequencer and the song switches to that artist's melody on the fly — the beat never stops, so you can audition styles seamlessly.

16

Generate an artist sample

Generate Music renders a clip in the chosen artist's style, picks a fresh tone and melody, and names it after one of that artist's real top songs — then drops it in your sample list.

17

Upload your own audio

In Sample Studio, upload a WAV or MP3. It saves to your browser and drops straight onto the timeline at bar 1, so every upload plays in sync with the song.

18

Record a sample

Rec Sample captures whatever the studio is playing into a new slot with its own fader and channel — perfect for bouncing a loop and re-sequencing it.

19

Record your microphone

Record MIC captures your microphone straight into a sample slot, so you can add vocals, claps or found sounds and treat them like any other instrument.

20

Bend pitch & time

The toolbar's Pitch and Stretch sliders drive PhaseVocoder.js: shift pitch by semitones while keeping length, or stretch length while keeping pitch — on samples and the export.

21

Balance the Mixer

Open the Mixer to set a fader, mute and solo per track. Solo a part to focus on it, or pull faders to blend the arrangement.

22

Sculpt with the Effects Rack

The master Effects Rack under the piano adds Reverb, Delay, Feedback, Chorus, Drive, Filter, Crush and Swing — the whole mix flows through it before your speakers.

23

Trim & expand clips

On the timeline, drag either edge of a block inward to shorten it, or outward to grow it — drag past the end and the song adds bars to make room automatically.

24

Move, copy & paste

Grab a block's header to slide it, use its copy button to duplicate, or select it and press Ctrl+C then Ctrl+V — the notes travel with the block.

25

Set the song length

In the Loop Sequencer, the Length menu sizes a new random song to Auto or 8–48 bars, and Add 4 Bars / Remove 4 resize the current one.

26

Export WAV or MP3

Render the whole song — or a single sample with Export Sample — to a downloadable WAV or MP3, encoded right in your browser.

27

Save & reload projects

Save Project writes every setting and track to a file; Load Project brings it all back. Recorded samples stay saved in your browser, ready for next time.

28

Scratch any instrument like a DJ

Select any instrument — piano, drums, a synth or a recorded sample — and the Scratch Sampler vinyl above Amp Tone loads that instrument's own sound. Spin the record round to scratch it — it turns to follow your hand and reverses when you turn it back. Let go while spinning for a smooth free spin.

29

Share your settings

Hit Export Settings in the top bar to save your tempo, tone, amp, effects and pitch/stretch as a JSON file. Send it to a friend — they just Load Project to match your sound.
