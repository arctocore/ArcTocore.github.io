MusicBand Studio Pro — how it works
Overview

A full band studio
that runs in your browser.

MusicBand Studio Pro turns a single web page into a playable studio: real sampled instruments, a step sequencer, a mixer with a master effects rack, whole-song generation in the style of famous artists, sample recording, phase-vocoder pitch & stretch, and one-click WAV / MP3 export. This guide walks through the whole flow — from pressing Start Audio to exporting a finished track.

Real instruments

Drums, guitars, piano, strings, brass, woodwinds, organ and synth keys — streamed as real audio samples with synth fallbacks.

Generate songs

Randomize a full arrangement in the style of 30 featured artists, each with its own signature melody, groove and progression.

Mix & shape

A per-track mixer plus a master effects rack, sound types, amp tones and guitar distortion sculpt the sound.

Phase vocoder

Shift pitch and stretch time independently on samples and on the final export, powered by PhaseVocoder.js.

Quick Start

From silence to a finished track

Follow these steps in order — the studio is designed to be used from top to bottom.

1

Start the audio engine

Click Start Audio in the top toolbar. Browsers only allow sound after a click, so this unlocks the engine and fades the master in cleanly.

2

Pick an instrument

Choose a card in Band Instruments. Play it with the on-screen piano, the keyboard keys A–K, or the chord pads.

3

Generate or build a song

Press Randomize Song to create a full arrangement in an artist's style, or build your own by adding parts to the Loop Sequencer.

4

Arrange on the timeline

Every track is a block you can trim, expand and move, just like clips in a modern DAW.

5

Shape the sound

Balance levels in the Mixer, dial in the Effects Rack, and adjust global Pitch and Stretch in the toolbar.

6

Record, export & save

Record a performance, export a WAV or MP3, and save the whole project to a file so you can reload it later.

Interface Tour

The panels, one by one

The studio is organised into collapsible panels. Here is what each one does.

Top toolbar

Transport (Play, Stop, Record, Click, Clear), Save / Load Project, and global sliders: Tempo, Master, Octave, plus phase-vocoder Pitch & Stretch.

Band Instruments

Real sampled instruments plus a Sound Type selector, per-instrument amp tone, and a distortion selector for electric guitar.

Song Presets

One-tap patterns that load a ready-made groove so you can start playing straight away.

Selected & Piano

The performance area: playable pads, a responsive piano keyboard, Record Sample, Record Mic, Generate Music, and Export Sample.

Effects Rack

The master effects, sitting under the piano: Reverb, Delay, Feedback, Chorus, Drive, Filter, Crush and Swing.

Mixer & Sequencer

A full-width mixer with a fader, mute and solo per track, and a bar-based Loop Sequencer for the whole song.

Make Music

Generate, in the style of the greats

MusicBand can build whole songs and short samples for you. Nothing is copied from real recordings — each of the 130+ artists is an original blend of tempo, groove and chord progression, and every render pulls a fresh lead melody from a 200-tune bank so you never hear the same song twice.

Randomize Song

Pick a style, or leave it on “Surprise Me” to pull a random title from the whole list of 130+ artists and genres. The studio lays down drums, bass, chords, an optional pad and a fresh lead melody across the timeline.

“Surprise Me” never repeats the last title, and the lead melody is drawn anew every time — so each Randomize is a different song.

Generate Music sample

Generate Music renders a short clip for the selected instrument in the Style you choose beside Amp Tone — a specific artist or “Surprise Me” for a random one — using a fresh melody every time, and saves it as a new sample you can drop on the timeline.

It respects your chosen Sound Type, amp tone and guitar distortion, so the sample matches your live sound.

Sound Types

Swap an instrument's real timbre for a synth voice — electronic, techno, synth pad, retro, FM, supersaw and more.

Amp Tones

Drive plus a three-band EQ, with 34 voicings from Classic Rock and Jazz to Metal, Djent and Lo-Fi.

Guitar Distortion

The electric guitar adds distortion of sizes, angles, distances and directions — drive, bit-crush, space and stereo movement.

Timeline & Regions

Arrange like a modern DAW

The Loop Sequencer is a bar-based timeline. Every part drops as a block sized to its own sound length — a phrase, a groove or a sample clip — so you can shape the arrangement the way you would in Soundtrap or GarageBand.

Trim

Drag either edge of a block inward to shorten which part of the track plays.

Expand

Drag an edge outward — past the end and the song grows to make room automatically.

Move

Grab the block's header to slide the whole part — its notes travel with it.

Copy & Paste

Duplicate a block with its copy button, or select it and press Ctrl+C then Ctrl+V.

Add or remove bars, set a per-clip tempo, and edit any step cell by clicking it. While a song is still auto-generated, the top Play button spins up a fresh random title each time — as soon as you edit a block, it becomes yours and Play just plays it.

Record & Phase Vocoder

Capture and reshape sound

Record the studio's own output or your microphone into a sample slot, then bend it with the global phase vocoder.

Record samples

Rec Sample captures whatever the studio is playing; Record Mic captures your microphone. Uploaded WAV files work too. Each recording lands in its own slot with a fader and channel.

Pitch & Stretch

The toolbar's Pitch and Stretch sliders drive PhaseVocoder.js: Pitch shifts by semitones while keeping length, and Stretch changes length while keeping pitch — applied to your samples and to the final export.

Export & Save

Take your track with you

WAV & MP3

Render the whole song — or a single sample — to a downloadable WAV or MP3, encoded right in your browser.

Save Project

Save every setting and track to a project file: tempo, effects, sound type, amp tone, distortion and the timeline.

Load Project

Reload a project file any time to pick up exactly where you left off. Recorded audio is stored in your browser.

Step-by-Step Tutorial

Make your first track in seven steps

Follow this walkthrough from a silent studio to a finished, exported song. Each step points to the exact control on the main page.

1

Start the audio engine

Click Start Audio in the top transport to unlock the browser's sound. The status light turns to Ready, and Play, Record and the keyboard come alive.

2

Pick an instrument

Choose a sound in the Band Instruments rack — grand piano, drums, guitar, strings, brass or a synth. Your pick drives the piano keys, the pads and the Generate Music button.

3

Build a loop

Press Randomize Song for a full arrangement in a Style you choose, or play the on-screen piano and pads. Generate Music renders a melodic clip in the selected Style and Sound Type.

4

Arrange in the timeline

Drag a block to move it, drag either edge to trim or expand, and set a per-clip tempo. Duplicate a part with its copy button, or select it and press Ctrl+C then Ctrl+V to paste.

5

Keep it tight

Clips only sound inside their block. A sample now stops at the end of its region instead of ringing out over the empty bars that follow, so only the active music plays.

6

Record & shape the sound

Hit Record to capture a take over the loop; stopping opens the Export Music window. Shape the tone with Amp Tone, Distortion, the Effects Rack and the global Pitch / Stretch sliders.

7

Export or save

Download the finished mix as WAV or MP3, or Save Project to keep every setting and track so you can pick up right where you left off.
